Oxygen Concentrators

PSA stands for Pressure Swing Adsorption. The concentrator draws in room air (~78% nitrogen, ~21% oxygen), pushes it through zeolite molecular sieve columns that trap nitrogen molecules, and delivers the remaining oxygen-enriched air (93%±3% purity) through a flow meter to the patient. The two sieve columns alternate — while one adsorbs nitrogen, the other regenerates — allowing continuous, uninterrupted oxygen flow. No cylinders, no refills, no deliveries needed.

Yes. HomeMedix concentrators (KX and KV) are engineered and tested for continuous 24-hour, 7-day-a-week operation. The molecular sieves are rated for 20,000+ operating hours and carry a 5-year sieve warranty. The only routine maintenance required is monthly filter cleaning, which takes about 2 minutes.

Both models deliver identical oxygen purity (93%±3%) and flow range (1–5 LPM). The key differences are form factor and portability. The KX is the standard home model with slightly higher build robustness. The KV is the compact variant — smaller, lighter, with a carry handle and wheels — ideal for smaller rooms or patients who travel between locations. If you have the space, the KX is preferred; if portability matters, choose the KV.

Both KX and KV operate at ≤45 dB(A) at 1-metre distance — roughly equivalent to a quiet conversation or a library. Most users find they can sleep in the same room without disturbance. Placing the device on a rubber mat and ensuring clear ventilation space (30 cm minimum on all sides) further reduces perceived noise.

Oxygen concentrators run on mains power and do not have built-in battery backup. For patients who are oxygen-dependent, we strongly recommend a UPS/inverter with at least 2 hours backup as a precaution during outages. Alternatively, keeping a small backup oxygen cylinder for emergencies is a common practice we advise clinically dependent users to adopt.

Nebulizers

All standard nebulization drugs can be used: bronchodilators (Salbutamol, Ipratropium), corticosteroids (Budesonide / Pulmicort), mucolytics (Acetylcysteine), and normal saline. The HM Pro mesh nebulizer is also compatible with suspension formulations. Always use drugs as prescribed by your physician. Oils and undiluted drug concentrates should be avoided unless specifically formulated for nebulization.

After every use: Disassemble the medication cup, mouthpiece/mask, and tubing. Rinse with warm water and air dry on a clean cloth. Daily: Soak removable parts in distilled water for 5 minutes. Weekly: Disinfect with diluted white vinegar (1:3 vinegar:water) for 20 minutes, then rinse and air dry. For the HM Pro mesh: after each use, run distilled water through the cup for 30 seconds to flush the mesh — this prevents drug crystallisation in the apertures.

The MistyNeb is a compressor nebulizer — it uses compressed air to break liquid drug into aerosol. It's robust, handles all drug viscosities including thick suspensions, and is lower cost. It operates at ~60 dB and takes 10–15 minutes per session. The HM Pro uses vibrating mesh technology, operates at ≤30 dB (virtually silent), completes a session in under 7 minutes, and achieves 99% drug utilisation vs 30–50% for compressors. The HM Pro is the premium upgrade for faster, quieter, more efficient therapy.

CPAP & BiPAP

A sleep study (polysomnography or home sleep apnea test) and prescription are required for CPAP/BiPAP therapy in India, as the pressure settings must be set by a qualified physician based on your AHI (Apnea Hypopnea Index) result. HomeMedix recommends against self-prescribing PAP therapy. Please consult a pulmonologist or sleep specialist. We can refer you to sleep clinics if needed — contact us.

CPAP (Continuous Positive Airway Pressure) delivers a single constant pressure throughout both inhalation and exhalation — the standard treatment for Obstructive Sleep Apnea (OSA). BiPAP (Bi-level PAP) delivers two distinct pressures: higher IPAP on inhalation and lower EPAP on exhalation, making it easier to breathe out against pressure. BiPAP is prescribed for patients who can't tolerate CPAP, or those with central sleep apnea, OHS (Obesity Hypoventilation Syndrome), or requiring pressures above 15 cmH₂O.

Yes. HomeMedix CPAP/BiPAP devices support universal voltage (100–240V, 50/60Hz), making them suitable for international travel. They are also classified as medical devices by most airlines and may be used onboard with prior notification. Check with your specific airline for their cabin use policy. We recommend carrying the device as hand luggage to avoid checked baggage mishandling.

Anti-Bedsore Mattresses

AirQuilt is an active alternating pressure air mattress — its pump cycles between inflating and deflating air cells every 6 minutes, constantly shifting pressure points to stimulate circulation. Best for prevention and Stage 1–2 pressure ulcers. Has a quiet (28 dB) motor. AquaQuilt is a passive water mattress — no motor or electricity. Its three water chambers distribute body weight evenly via Pascal's principle. Better for Stage 3–4 severe ulcers where any movement must be minimised. Completely silent.

Yes. The AirQuilt is designed as an overlay system — it sits on top of a regular hospital or home mattress (up to 20 cm thick) without any special bed frame required. Place the air cells face-up, connect the pump tubing, and lay the patient directly on the air cell surface. A thin cotton sheet can be placed over the air cells without significantly reducing effectiveness.

Fill each chamber using the supplied hand pump and distilled water (not tap water, which can cause algae growth). Fill to the recommended level marked on each chamber — typically 70–80% capacity. Add the included water treatment solution to each chamber annually to prevent microbial growth. Check and top up water levels monthly. The outer PVC cover can be wiped with mild disinfectant.
