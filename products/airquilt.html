Understanding the Problem

What Is a Bedsore — and Why Does It Matter?

The Silent Threat to Bed-Ridden Patients

When a patient lies in the same position for hours, prolonged pressure cuts off blood supply to skin and underlying tissue. Within just 2–3 hours, this can cause pressure ulcers (bedsores) — painful wounds that can escalate to serious infections, significantly increasing treatment costs and hospital stay.

The standard solution of manual repositioning every 2 hours is labour-intensive and often impractical in home care settings or understaffed wards.

  • ✓ Pressure ulcers affect up to 30% of hospitalised patients in India
  • ✓ Healing a Stage 4 ulcer can cost 10× more than preventing it
  • ✓ Manual repositioning is often missed during night shifts

The AirQuilt solves this automatically — around the clock.
