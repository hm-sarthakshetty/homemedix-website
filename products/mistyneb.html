What Is a Nebulizer?

Turning Liquid Medicine Into Healing Mist

Direct-to-Lung Drug Delivery

A nebulizer converts liquid medication into a fine aerosol mist that is inhaled directly into the lungs. This delivers the drug exactly where it's needed — bypassing the digestive system, acting faster, and requiring smaller doses compared to tablets or syrups.

Nebulizers are prescribed for asthma, COPD, bronchitis, cystic fibrosis, croup, and other respiratory conditions. They are especially valuable for young children, elderly patients, and anyone who struggles to use inhalers correctly.

  • ✓Works with bronchodilators, corticosteroids, antibiotics, and saline
  • ✓No technique required — patient simply breathes normally through the mask
  • ✓Effective for infants, children, and adults who cannot use pressurised inhalers
  • ✓Used in hospitals, clinics, and home settings
The Technology

How the MistyNeb Works

1

Load Medication

Liquid medication (prescribed by your doctor) is poured into the 6ml nebuliser cup. The cup connects to the device.

2

Compressor Powers Up

The heavy-duty motor drives compressed air through a narrow jet, creating a Venturi effect inside the nebulizer cup.

3

Fine Mist Generated

High-velocity air breaks the liquid into microscopic droplets (2–5 micron size) — fine enough to reach deep into the bronchioles.

4

Patient Inhales Mist

Patient breathes normally through the adult or child mask. Full treatment typically takes 10–15 minutes.
