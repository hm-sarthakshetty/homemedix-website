Understanding the Device

What is a Mesh Nebulizer — and Why Does It Matter?

Not all nebulizers are created equal. The technology inside determines how much of your medication actually reaches your lungs.

The Problem with Conventional Nebulizers

Traditional compressor-based and jet nebulizers work by forcing compressed air through a liquid drug to create aerosol. The process is loud (60–80 dB), slow (10–20 min per session), and wastes 50–70% of the drug as exhaled mist or residue left in the cup.

How Vibrating Mesh Technology Works

The HM Pro uses a precision-drilled palladium mesh with 1,000+ micro-apertures, each just 3 µm wide. A piezoelectric actuator vibrates the mesh at ultrasonic frequency, extruding drug particles through those apertures as consistent 0.3 µm aerosol — the ideal size to penetrate deep into the alveolar lung tissue.

Why Particle Size Changes Everything

Studies show particles between 1–5 µm deposit in the lower airways where most respiratory conditions occur. The HM Pro's 0.3 µm MMAD ensures maximum bronchodilator and corticosteroid deposition precisely where it's needed — delivering superior clinical outcomes with smaller doses.

Step-by-Step

How the HM Pro Delivers Your Medication

A silent, 7-minute nebulization cycle from fill to finish.

01

Load the Medication Cup

Fill the 7 ml medication cup with your prescribed drug solution — bronchodilators, corticosteroids, antibiotics, or saline. The HM Pro is compatible with all standard nebulization drugs including Budesonide, Salbutamol, and Ipratropium.

02

Mesh Extrusion Begins

The piezoelectric transducer vibrates the palladium mesh at ~128 kHz. Drug solution is drawn up by capillary action and extruded through 1,000+ micro-apertures as a consistent stream of 0.3 µm MMAD aerosol particles — completely silently.

03

Deep Lung Deposition

The fine aerosol flows through the mouthpiece or mask at a gentle velocity, carrying medication past the vocal cords and into the bronchioles and alveoli. The slow flow rate prevents oropharyngeal deposition — meaning more drug reaches your lungs, not your throat.

04

Session Complete in <7 Minutes

The device automatically stops when the medication cup is empty. An average 4 ml drug load nebulizes in under 7 minutes — compared to 15–20 minutes for compressor nebulizers. Clean the mesh with distilled water and it's ready for the next session.
