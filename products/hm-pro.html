Understanding the Device

Compressor Nebulization for Clinical Environments

The HM Pro is engineered for environments where the nebulizer runs continuously — back-to-back patients, multi-session days, and clinical workflows that demand reliability.

Copper Motor for Continuous Duty

The HM Pro uses a copper-wound motor — the same motor technology found in industrial-grade compressors. Copper dissipates heat more efficiently than aluminium, which means the motor maintains consistent performance through extended operation without thermal throttling or overheating shutdowns.

Oversized Enclosure for Ventilation

The larger body isn't aesthetic — it's functional. More internal volume means better airflow around the compressor, improved heat dissipation, and longer continuous run times. The enclosure is designed for the thermal demands of hospital and clinic settings where the device may run for hours each day.

Built-In Fuse Protection

Integrated fuse safeguards protect the motor from power surges and overcurrent events — a critical safety feature in Indian clinical environments where voltage fluctuations are common. The fuse is user-replaceable, minimising downtime.

Step-by-Step

How the HM Pro Delivers Medication

Reliable jet nebulization, session after session.

01

Load the Medication Cup

Fill the medication cup with your prescribed drug solution — bronchodilators, corticosteroids, antibiotics, mucolytics, or saline. The HM Pro is compatible with all standard aqueous nebulization drugs including Salbutamol, Budesonide, Ipratropium, and Levosalbutamol.

02

Compressor Generates Airflow

The copper motor drives compressed air at 6–8 LPM through a narrow jet orifice inside the nebulizer cup. This creates a Venturi effect that draws liquid medication upward and shatters it into a fine aerosol mist with particle sizes in the 2–5 µm range.

03

Aerosol Reaches the Airways

The aerosolised medication flows through the mouthpiece or mask, carrying drug particles past the upper airways and into the bronchioles where respiratory conditions are treated. The 2–5 µm MMAD is the clinically established range for effective lower airway deposition.

04

Ready for the Next Patient

After each session, replace the medication cup and mouthpiece/mask for the next patient. The copper motor and oversized enclosure are designed for back-to-back use — the HM Pro handles continuous clinical workflows without needing cool-down periods between sessions.
