When Standard Air Mattresses Aren't Enough

Water: Nature's Perfect Pressure Distributor

Pascal's principle tells us that water distributes pressure equally in all directions. This is the principle behind the AquaQuilt — using water's natural physics to eliminate the high-pressure points that cause tissue damage in bed-ridden patients.

The AquaQuilt is typically recommended for high-risk patients — those with existing Stage 3–4 pressure ulcers, patients with very low body weight, or cases where alternating pressure air mattresses have not been sufficient.

The Technology

How the AquaQuilt Works

1

Three Chambers Filled

The mattress has three independent water chambers — head/torso, mid-section, and lower body — each filled to the prescribed level.

2

Body Weight Distributed

Water responds to the body's shape in real-time, distributing weight across the entire surface — including bony prominences like heels, sacrum, and shoulders.

3

Tissue Perfusion Maintained

With no pressure hotspots, blood can flow freely through skin and subcutaneous tissue — even in patients who cannot be repositioned.

4

Independent Zone Control

Clinicians can adjust water level in each chamber independently to accommodate specific wounds, surgical sites, or patient preferences.

The AirQuilt is an active alternating pressure air mattress with a motor that cycles every 10–12 minutes — best for prevention and Stage 1–2 pressure ulcers. The AquaQuilt is a passive water bed with three independent chambers that distribute body weight evenly using Pascal's principle — no motor, no electricity, completely silent. The AquaQuilt is preferred for Stage 3–4 severe ulcers and high-risk patients where any mechanical movement must be minimised.

The included water treatment solution should be added to each chamber annually to prevent microbial growth. Water levels should be checked and topped up monthly.

The AquaQuilt is constructed from medical-grade PVC that is durable and designed for long-term hospital use. Under normal use, leaks are extremely rare. The PVC surface is compatible with hospital-grade disinfectants for regular cleaning. In the unlikely event of a puncture, the three-chamber design means only one section is affected, and the mattress can be repaired.
